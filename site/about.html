§ About

Eight years
at Predikherenlei.

Allusion BV was founded in 2018 by Paul De Cannière as a personal operating vehicle. Today it's three practices under one roof — Ventures, Infrastructure, and Lab — sharing a building, a balance sheet, and a set of convictions about what foundational systems look like.

§ 01 · Story

From cohousing & korte keten
to sovereign infrastructure.

Allusion began in 2018 as the vehicle through which Paul supported organizations working on cohousing, short food chains, inclusion, and alternative energy. Patient capital, advisory work, the occasional bridge loan.

In 2019 the first venture investments went out. In May 2021 we onboarded as the first Belgian Node Provider for the Internet Computer — a decision that put real hardware behind the conviction that decentralized infrastructure was about to matter. We've been operating that infrastructure ever since.

The Lab pillar — research, experiments, long-horizon bets — grew out of the operational practice. Some of it became Aviate Labs. Some of it became ICP Belgium. Some of it stays internal as the kind of work that doesn't fit a portfolio thesis but feeds everything else.

The model is unusual: an operating company, an investment vehicle, and a research lab sharing a balance sheet. It works because each pillar makes the others more honest. Founders we back become operating partners. The hardware tells us what's actually hard. The lab keeps us from getting stale.

§ 03 · Three practices, one balance sheet

Ventures. Infrastructure. Lab.

Ventures · 13 portfolio companies

First checks into pre-seed and seed founders in digital infrastructure, health tech, and energy. €50K — €400K opening ticket; follow-ons up to €5M.

Infrastructure · 42 nodes since 2021

Internet Computer Node Provider since May 2021. Three Belgian data centers, three subnets, full redundancy. Aviate Labs runs the monitoring layer.

Lab · Long-horizon bets

Research, experiments, and community work that doesn't fit a portfolio thesis — HORUS maritime consortium, ICP Belgium, brand systems, the occasional pamphlet.

The shared discipline

Patient capital, operational depth, and the willingness to say no. We pass more often than we invest — and we say why.

§ 04 · Brand & visual identity

After Mazois, 1812.

The Allusion identity is distilled from the engraved frontispiece of François Mazois, Les Ruines de Pompei (Paris, Firmin Didot, 1812). Five colors, two typefaces, one disciplined system — built to age well in correspondence, presentation, signage, and the occasional building.
